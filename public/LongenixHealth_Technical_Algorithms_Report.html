Biological Age Calculation Algorithms

1. Phenotypic Age Algorithm

Levine et al. 2018 Mortality Validated

Scientific Foundation:

Published in Aging journal by Levine et al. (2018). Uses 9 clinical biomarkers to predict mortality risk and biological age. Strong correlation with healthspan and longevity outcomes.

Mathematical Formula:

Mortality Score =
-0.0336 × Albumin + 0.0095 × Creatinine + 0.1953 × Glucose
+ 0.0954 × ln(CRP + 1) - 0.0120 × Lymphocyte%
+ 0.0268 × AlkPhos + 0.0554 × WBC + 0.0026 × MCV
+ 0.3306 × RDW
Phenotypic Age = 141.50 + ln(-0.00553 × ln(1 - e^(mortality_score))) / 0.09165

Required Biomarkers (9):

  • • Albumin (g/dL)
  • • Creatinine (mg/dL)
  • • Glucose (mg/dL)
  • • C-Reactive Protein (mg/L)
  • • Lymphocyte Percentage (%)
  • • Alkaline Phosphatase (U/L)
  • • White Blood Cells (K/μL)
  • • Mean Cell Volume (fL)
  • • Red Cell Distribution Width (%)

Implementation Logic:

  • • Minimum 6/9 biomarkers required
  • • Age bounds: 0-120 years
  • • Fallback to simplified calculation if insufficient data
  • • Logarithmic transformation for CRP
  • • Exponential mortality-to-age conversion

2. Klemera-Doubal Method (KDM)

Klemera & Doubal 2006 Linear Regression

Scientific Foundation:

Linear regression approach using multiple biomarkers with age-correlation coefficients. Provides weighted biological age based on biomarker deviation from age-expected values.

Mathematical Formula:

KDM Age = Σ(wi × (CA + (Xi - μi) / (σi × ri))) / Σwi
Where: wi = ri², CA = chronological age, Xi = biomarker value, μi = population mean, σi = standard deviation, ri = age correlation coefficient

Biomarker Coefficients:

• Systolic BP: r=0.4, μ=120, σ=20
• Total Cholesterol: r=0.2, μ=200, σ=40
• Glucose: r=0.3, μ=90, σ=20
• Creatinine: r=0.35, μ=1.0, σ=0.3
• Albumin: r=-0.25, μ=4.0, σ=0.5
• Hemoglobin: r=-0.15, μ=14, σ=2
• WBC: r=0.1, μ=7, σ=2

Implementation Details:

  • • Minimum 3 biomarkers required
  • • Z-score standardization of values
  • • Weighted regression approach
  • • Age bounds: 0-120 years
  • • High correlation with chronological age (r>0.9)

Composite Algorithm Rule-Based

Algorithm Logic:

Rule-based system that adjusts chronological age based on metabolic biomarker deviations from optimal ranges. Focuses on glucose metabolism, lipid profile, and body composition.

Adjustment Rules:

Glucose:
• >126 mg/dL: +5 years
• 101-126 mg/dL: +2 years
• <70 mg/dL: +1 year
HbA1c:
• >6.5%: +5 years
• 5.8-6.5%: +3 years
HDL Cholesterol:
• <40 mg/dL: +2 years
• >60 mg/dL: -2 years

Additional Factors:

• >200 mg/dL: +2 years
• <100 mg/dL: -1 year

• >160 mg/dL: +3 years
• <100 mg/dL: -1 year
BMI Categories:
• >30: +3 years (obese)
• 25-30: +1 year (overweight)

Disease Risk Assessment Algorithms

1. ASCVD Cardiovascular Risk

AHA/ACC 2018 C-statistic: 0.71-0.75

Clinical Application:

10-year atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk using pooled cohort equations. Predicts risk of myocardial infarction, coronary death, or stroke in adults 40-79 years.

Risk Score Components:

Risk Score = Σ(β × ln(risk_factor))
• Age coefficient × ln(age)
• Total cholesterol coefficient × ln(total_chol)
• HDL cholesterol coefficient × ln(hdl_chol)
• Systolic BP coefficient × ln(sbp)
• Smoking status (binary)
• Diabetes status (binary)
10-year Risk = 1 - S₀(t)^exp(risk_score - mean_score)

Gender-Specific Coefficients:

Male:
• Age: 12.344
• Total Chol: 11.853
• HDL Chol: -7.990
Female:
• Age: 17.114
• Total Chol: -1.499
• HDL Chol: 1.957

Risk Stratification:

• <5%: Low risk
• 5-7.5%: Borderline risk
• 7.5-20%: Intermediate risk
• >20%: High risk
• Age range: 40-79 years
• Population: US adults

2. FINDRISC Diabetes Risk

Lindström et al. 2003 AUC: 0.85

Algorithm Purpose:

Finnish Diabetes Risk Score for predicting 10-year Type 2 diabetes risk. Point-based system using demographic, anthropometric, and lifestyle factors.

Scoring System:

Age:
• <45 years: 0 points
• 45-54 years: 2 points
• 55-64 years: 3 points
• >64 years: 4 points
BMI:
• <25: 0 points
• 25-30: 1 point
• >30: 3 points

Risk Interpretation:

• <7 points: Low risk (1%)
• 7-11 points: Slightly elevated (4%)
• 12-14 points: Moderate (17%)
• 15-20 points: High (33%)
• >20 points: Very high (50%)
• Maximum: 26 points

Hallmarks of Aging - Detailed Calculation Algorithms

Comprehensive assessment of 12 fundamental aging mechanisms based on López-Otín et al. framework, updated with 2023 research. Each hallmark uses specific biomarkers and lifestyle factors with evidence-based scoring algorithms.

Overall Aging Assessment Algorithm

López-Otín et al. 2023 Multi-Modal

Master Calculation:

Overall Aging Score = Σ(hallmark_impact_percentage) / 12 hallmarks
Biological Age Acceleration = (overall_score - 50) × 0.2
Primary Concerns = hallmarks where impact > 60% OR risk_level ≥ 'high'
Confidence Level = minimum(all_hallmark_confidence_levels)

Hallmarks of Health - Optimization Algorithms

Comprehensive assessment of 8 positive health domains focusing on optimization rather than disease risk. Each domain uses evidence-based biomarkers and lifestyle factors to calculate health span potential and optimization opportunities.

Health Optimization Master Algorithm

Positive Health Model Multi-Domain

Master Calculations:

Overall Health Score = Σ(domain_score) / 8 domains
Health Span Projection = base_life_expectancy - current_age + health_bonus
Health Bonus = (health_score - 50) × 0.3 years per 10 points
Primary Strengths = domains scoring ≥80%
Optimization Opportunities = domains scoring <70%

8 Health Optimization Domains:

  • • Metabolic Health: Glucose, lipids, BMI optimization
  • • Cardiovascular Fitness: BP, lipids, physical activity
  • • Cognitive Reserve: Brain health, neuroprotection
  • • Immune Resilience: Inflammatory balance, immune function
  • • Physical Performance: Strength, mobility, functional capacity
  • • Sleep Quality: Sleep optimization and recovery
  • • Stress Resilience: Stress management and adaptation
  • • Nutritional Status: Nutrient optimization and absorption

Optimization Levels:

• ≥85%: Optimal (exceptional health)
• 75-84%: Excellent (high performance)
• 65-74%: Good (healthy range)
• 50-64%: Fair (improvement needed)
• <50%: Poor (intervention required)
